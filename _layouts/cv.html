Full-stack Developer @ Siemens Gamesa FREELANCE

access_time Jan. 2023 - Jan. 2024
work Siemens Gamesa (on behalf of Kunlabora)
location_on Leuven, Belgium. Cuxhaven, Germany

Minimizing the air-gap of offshore wind turbines.

Siemens Gamesa is one of the leaders in renewable energy and pioneered the offshore wind industry. Since then, both the use and size of wind turbines have scaled immensely. Producing and assembling these wind turbines requires the finest engineering and highest precision.

To ensure optimal yield and reduce maintenance overhead, minimizing the air gap between the rotor and stator is vital. Therefore, the assembly process is measurement-driven; each work center is equipped with their own specialized measurement devices.

As a member of the designated Kunlabora software development team, we were tasked with creating a central coordinator (named MWC). It mediates the communication between the involved work centers through well-defined protocols, schematics, and APIs. MWC aggregates the measurements and channels them to subsequent work centers, enabling these work centers and their operators to achieve the highest precision. When each link in the assembly chain is optimized, so is the final assembled turbine.

Additionally, MWC is built to offer support for managing and planning production orders, for manually intervening, overriding or rolling back steps in the assembly process (failsafes), for monitoring the assembly process and its current progress, for monitoring the state of the MWC infrastructure, and for designing and defining—at runtime—the actors and exchanged data involved in the assembly process of a particular wind turbine.

Finally, we had full responsibility for configuring and installing the entire, high-availability, infrastructure on which MWC runs.

A truly unique project, experience, and team.
